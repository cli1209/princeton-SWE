SWE Elections 2004

Tuesday, March 2, 2004

Society of Women Engineers, Princeton University Student Chapter

 

Welcome Princeton Women Engineers! 

          We are glad to hear that you are considering running for a position as a SWE officer.  We are hoping to elect 8 officers this year.  Although it may seem like a lot of officers, we found that having this many officers made everything go more smoothly.   Between the busy schedule of a Princeton Engineer and the other activities we are involved with on campus it is best to have many officers so that even if everyone cannot make it each week, events can be pulled off without a hitch.

          We would definitely like to see involvement of engineers from the freshman and sophomore classes.  Having a variety of ages and majors gives a broad base for finding the needs of women engineers and advertising the events.  It is not required that you are currently a member of the national SWE to become an officer although next June you would be expected to join/renew ($20 for most, and free for presidents).  

          The elections will be held after the study break on Tuesday March 2.  You don’t have to worry about making a long speech; it will be pretty short and informal.  Just a little about who you are and why you want the position and why you are qualified will do fine.

   The positions are described below, although all of the officers usually help out with each event in some way. 

Co-presidents (2): to oversee the activity planning.  The presidents are responsible for ensuring that everything that must be done gets done.  They generally direct the meetings.  They also tend to take on many of the one-time-only small tasks because it is easier to do than delegate.  Presidents receive all of the information from the treasurer, from the NJ section of SWE, from the officers and from the office and are responsible for keeping track of all the information and relaying it a necessary. 

Vice President: Communication with the NJ section of SWE, the national SWE conferences and other local chapters that hold events.  They also help with activities like all other officers. 

Treasurer: The treasurer manages the account held with Sharon Kulik in Dean Bogucki’s office.  The treasurer must get Purchase Order numbers for large purchases and collect the receipts from officers for the smaller purchases so that they can be reimbursed.  The treasurer also writes up the annual budget in the beginning of the year to request funding and keeps track of the amount left in the account throughout the year.

Secretary: The secretary is responsible for drafting letters when necessary.  Letters of thanks are sent to donors, emails are sent to the Society and to the engineering school in general, and communication with the NJ section of SWE. 

Activities Chairs (3):  Take over responsibility for seeing through with activities.  Examples include study breaks, High school colloquium (or one of the activities with in the colloquium), speakers, section meetings, t-shirts etc. 

I hope you find this information helpful.  If you have more questions, feel free to speak with the current officers before elections on Tuesday.  Take Care!

Can’t make it but want to run?  Email swe@princeton.edu with a statements and we will read it for you.
